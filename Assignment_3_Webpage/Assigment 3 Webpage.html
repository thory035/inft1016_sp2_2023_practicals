    Executive Summary

    This report reflects upon assignment 1 task topic, the emergence of cloud gaming and its impact on game development, which explored software and hardware shifts, and high-end content. These topics covered the potential effect it has on technological systems and the way it will influence the demands of consumers. This then determines the path of game developers and how they must customise and shape systems to cater towards their audiences. High quality content is not only visually appealing but brings users and immersive experience. Going hand in hand, software and hardware shifts determine the output of quality and in turn influence the response to user’s experience. Traditional methods of teaching have slowly changed by adapting to the introduction of built in smart boards, the normalised use of laptops assigned to students, and the growth of learning accompanied by these machineries. Cloud gaming has “revolutionised the field of networks and the way of utilising the resources remotely” (Krishna et al., 2017). This document will critique and analyse several credible state-of-the-art literatures, review popular applications regarding cloud gaming and how it has become, and will become, a solution for educational purposes. The use of game-related activities has shown to impact children, based on studies, revealed noticeable learning improvement (Chaarani et al., 2022). Traditional teaching methods may be affected in various ways from improving classroom engagement to fostering critical thinking skills. There issues that may impede on the utilization of cloud technology such as technical and quality limitations. Regarding Australian schools, they differ from the rest of the world as schools are “funded through a combination of Australian Government (Commonwealth) funding” (The Department of Education, n.d.). The forthcoming discussion will draw upon previous ideas to explore challenges and prospects of integrating cloud gaming in the future of education.
